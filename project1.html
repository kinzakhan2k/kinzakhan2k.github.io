Design for Recovery

Resilient Residence for Post Traumatic Stress Disorder

This project involves redesigning an existing home for a couple who experienced a traumatic car accident resulting in PTSD. The aim is to create a space conducive to their recovery, addressing their sensitivity to noise and aversion to bright red colors. The design focuses on an enclosed yet open and vibrant atmosphere, incorporating comforting colors and tranquil elements. Detailed construction drawings will guide the implementation of the design, ensuring a nurturing environment for the couple's healing journey.

The idea of open within enclosed is explored through design choices that use visibility from inside to outside while also maintaining privacy by not allowing the entrance to open on the street, rather, towards the back, creating an intimate and private zone. After entering the space, the inside utilizes outdoor greenery, blurring the lines between interior and exterior environments. A moss wall on exterior walls absorbs traffic noise, contributing to a serene atmosphere. Additionally, the inclusion of a water fountain brings continuous sounds of nature, enhancing the home's calming ambiance.

This home employs warm and uplifting materials like green and orange, as well as tinted wood flooring to create contrast and visual interest. Inside, courtyard spaces and plants are integrated at every step, fostering a connection with nature and promoting well-being. By incorporating these elements, the home encourages a secure and healing process to recovery, providing a sanctuary for the couple to find solace and peace.
